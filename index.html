About Terraria
This is an adventure game.
You can play on your own, with friends, or on a server.

Watch out it is highly addictive, as I have played over 1,700 hours.

Terraria is most widely acquired via Steam and is well worth the price tag, unless on console, I recommend you play with keyboard and mouse!

Getting Started
The first thing you will need to do is collect some wood, use the Copper Axe that is item 3 in your hotbar (although can be changed), and chop down a tree.
collect the wood, and then you are going to be ready to do some crafting.
Press escape and then craft yourself a WorkBench.
Place down the workbench and then craft the required items for a valid housing (housing suitable for an NPC).

Moving on
Once you have made your first valid housing you will want to make more.
Why?
Well each of the NPCs (That is non-Player Characters)will need their own living space, which has to be a valid housing. And as you advance through the game your are going to unlock more and more NPCs.
What next?
You are ready to explore, so some things you need to know

Night Time
Oh yes, when night falls and you are new to the game, you are going to want to keep yourself safe from Zombies and other nightly creatures (Hostile mobs that spawn at night). So it's best to close yourself into a Home or Cave on your first night, you will need torches and some wood to do this quickly. Do not worry too much about a door, just block yourself in and you can cut your way out in the morning.
However, night time need not be wasted, you can mine quite safely as long as you keep yourself blocked off from the Zombies. And as you collect new materials you will be able to craft your first items.

Torches require wood and Gel , you collect the Gel each time you defeat a slime (easiest in the day time, because they only spawn in day time, but hang around through the night just hopping about).
Tools you are best to collect materials like metal ore (which you need to smelt in a furnace) and then make metal tools. Note you can also make pretty decent tools out of Cactus if you find you are close to the desert.
Weapons are fashioned in a similar way to tools and the Cactus Sword is highly recommended as a much more effective weapon that the copper short sword that you got in your starter pack.
Armour is pretty important too!!! If you like to stay alive for longer, then craft some armour out of available materials and put it on (menu is located toward the right of your screen when having your inventory open). You will find as you go through the game that items made from better materials give more protection, and some complete sets give a whole set Buff (increase in certain stat or some form of bonus).
Health is always running down when we get attacked, injured, poisoned, or fall from too great a height. Keep healing potions that you find in Vases and Chests handy, and press the 'H' (every keybind can be rebound to anything you like!) key to heal.
Chests are all around the world, right-click a chest to open it and see what is inside, loot it and then mine the chest to take back to your base for storing all your treasure and materials, because whilst a 40 slot inventory seems large, it soon gets full.
Vases are plentiful and all over the surface and underground, smash them with Axe or Sword and collect the goodies from inside, these include Rope, Torches, Coins, Bombs, some weapons and Ammunition. They are a quick way to collect immediately needed materials.
Acorns you are collecting a lot of wood, chopping down a lot of trees, but please be responsible and replant the acorns, this will prevent the world becoming barren and provide plenty more wood when you need it later.
Mining by far the fastest way to progress in Terraria is to avoid spending pointless hours/days mining, but it is good fun, so I advise to run across the surface and access the underground where possible, via existing entrances and caves, and mine when you need to in order to make progress, collect materials and to avoid dangerous mobs.
